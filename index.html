Assignment 3: White Hat & Black Hat Visualizations

White Hat: Emissions of GHGs by Continent


These three visualizations were created by getting the tonnes of co2 equaivalent of different greenhouse gases in North America, Europe, and Asia. To ensure accurate representations of the greenhouse gas amounts the y-axis was kept on a linear scale, however the ability to highlight indiviual gases using the legend on the side. as well as being able to view the exact values for each year by hovering over the plot ensures data can be clearly extracted. Additionally, when filtering by continent, the values for each country were restricted to one continent (ex. Russia is included one in the European averages and not as part of Asia).

Black Hat: Emissions of GHGs by Country


Write-up for Black Hat Visual:
The first technique used is that the data was heavily filtered. Most of the low greenhouse gas (GHG) producing countries were filtered out so that a stark contrast between the low GHG producing countries and high GHG producing countries was not obvious. In addition to filtering the countries, the GHG metric was also filtered. Looking closely, one may notice that the US is only displaying its emission of CO2 while the other countries display their total GHG emissions. In this data set, the United States has the most amount of KG of CO2-equivalent produced and not by a small margin. To hide this fact, we chose to filter out the other GHGs and only represent CO2 since it has a value close to the total GHGs emission of other countries. Russia was an intentional addition since its GHG emissions are greater than that of the USâ€™ CO2 emissions so we can better paint the picture that the US is not the worse. The data was aggregated over the entire 29 year period (1998-2018) to show historical impact to hide what country has the most recent and relevant environmental impact in the current age. To hide the true scale of the data, the y-axis scale is logarithmic. Those who are not paying close attention may fail to realize that each marker is a difference of a factor of 10. Though the size of the bars may look similar in size, on a logarithmic scale, a small difference could be a considerable amount. The last technique is the graph name. We generalize with GHG for all countries when in reality, the US is based on only one of the GHGs rather than all of them. We leave in labels for GHG and CO2 so it is clear (if noticed) that we are not claiming that exclusively CO2 is not the total GHGs emission for the US.
